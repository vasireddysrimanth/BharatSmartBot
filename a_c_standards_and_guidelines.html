Standards and Guidelines

  • New employees must sign a service agreement and abide by the organizations rules, regulations, procedures and policies. They should also comply with the conditions specified in the agreement and appointment letter.
  • Upon joining candidates are required to submit the following documents;
      Original academic certificates which will be returned upon leaving Bharat Institutions.
      Two sets of self attested photocopies of certificates.
      One set of photocopies of certificates, without self attestation.

On joining the institution

  • Service Agreement and Adherence: - New appointees must execute a service agreement, adhering to organization's rules, regulations, procedures, policies, and agreement conditions.
  • Document Deposit on Joining: - Provide original academic certificates (to be returned at relieving). - Furnish self-attested photocopies of Relieving letters/Conduct Certificates from previous employers (bring originals for verification).

Transfer Policy

  • Mandatory Transfers: - Employees may be transferred as determined by the institution to different work locations or departments within Bharat Institutions.
  • Non-compliance Consequence: - Failure to adhere to transfer directives will be considered as an act of indiscipline.

Deputation Policy

  • Mandatory Deputation: - Employees may need to work on deputation as needed, at any location within Bharat Institutions.
  • Non-compliance Consequence: - Failing to comply with deputation requests will be treated as an act of indiscipline.

Exclusivity Policy

  • Exclusive Dedication: - Employees must dedicate themselves solely to services at Bharat Institutions.
  • Restriction on Other Work: - Employees cannot engage in other paid work, advisory roles, or business ventures during employment without written management permission.

Confidentiality of Salary Matters

  • Sensitivity of Details: - Salary/benefit information is highly confidential and should not be shared with other employees.
  • Maintaining Privacy: - Indulging in salary matters of fellow employees is discouraged to ensure confidentiality.

Contact Information Policy

  • Keeping Information Updated: - Employees must inform the institution promptly of any changes to their postal address, telephone number, fax, email, or other communication methods during their service period.
  • Communication Delivery: - Any communication sent to the last provided address is considered as successfully served.

ID Card Policy

  • Collecting ID Card: - Employees must obtain their ID card from the respective College/Unit HR department.
  • Mandatory ID Card Usage: - All employees are required to wear their ID card visibly within and outside the premises during working hours.

Dress Code Policy

  • Uniform Appearance: - All employees (Teaching and Non-Teaching) are required to maintain grooming and appearance standards suitable for various situations.
  • Formal Attire: - All employees are expected to dress in formal attire during their time at the institution
  • Non-compliance Consequence: - Failing to adhere to the dress code will be treated as an act of indiscipline.

Late Reporting Policy

  • Late Arrival Allowance: - Twice monthly, employees can report up to 30 minutes late without consequence.
  • Teaching Staff Exception: - If a teacher's first class is the first hour, any lateness is treated as half a day leave; otherwise, every third late arrival results in ½ day leave.
  • Initial Late Arrival: - Even for the first instance, lateness beyond 30 minutes is treated as ½ day leave.
